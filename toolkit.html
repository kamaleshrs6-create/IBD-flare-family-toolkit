📊 Symptom Tracker

Keep track of your child's IBD symptoms over time. Regular monitoring helps identify patterns and communicate effectively with your IBD team.

Why Track Symptoms?

  • Identifies symptom patterns and triggers
  • Helps your IBD team adjust treatment if needed
  • Provides early warning signs of flares
  • Tracks response to new medications
  • Improves communication during appointments

Abdominal Pain

Record location, severity (1-10 scale), and duration

Bowel Movements

Note frequency, consistency, and presence of blood/mucus

Energy Levels

Track fatigue and overall energy throughout the day

Appetite

Monitor food intake and appetite changes

Sleep Quality

Record sleep duration and quality

Other Symptoms

Joint pain, skin issues, or other associated symptoms

💩 Stool Diary

A detailed record of your child's bowel movements provides crucial information for managing IBD. This diary helps identify trends and treatment response.

What to Track

  • Date and time of each bowel movement
  • Stool consistency (Bristol Scale: hard to watery)
  • Blood presence and amount
  • Mucus presence
  • Urgency level
  • Associated symptoms (pain, cramping)

Frequency

How many times per day and at what times

Consistency

Use Bristol Scale (Type 1-7) to describe stool type

Blood & Mucus

Presence, color, and amount of blood or mucus

Urgency

How urgent was the need to have a bowel movement

Accompanying Symptoms

Pain level, cramping, or other symptoms

Medications Taken

Note any medications taken that day

🍎 Nutrition Tips & Guidance

Proper nutrition is essential for managing IBD and supporting your child's growth and development. Learn what to eat and what to avoid.

Nutrition During Flares

  • Stick to bland, easily digestible foods
  • Eat smaller, more frequent meals
  • Avoid high-fiber and fatty foods
  • Stay hydrated with fluids
  • Take nutritional supplements if recommended

✅ Good Food Choices

White bread, rice, pasta, chicken, fish, eggs, plain yogurt, bananas, applesauce, well-cooked vegetables

❌ Foods to Avoid

High-fiber cereals, whole wheat bread, raw vegetables, beans, nuts, fatty meats, spicy foods, dairy (if lactose intolerant)

💧 Hydration

Water, clear broths, diluted fruit juice, sports drinks with electrolytes

🍽️ Meal Planning

Small portions every 2-3 hours, keep a food diary to track triggers

📊 Nutritional Needs

May need supplements (iron, B12, calcium, vitamin D)

🧑‍⚕️ Consult Dietitian

Request referral to IBD dietitian for personalized plan

💊 Medication Information & Tracking

Keep detailed records of your child's medications, including dosage, frequency, side effects, and effectiveness. This information is vital for your IBD team.

Important Medication Details

  • Medication name and generic name
  • Dosage and frequency
  • Route of administration (oral, injection, infusion)
  • Prescription date and refill schedule
  • Side effects experienced
  • Effectiveness in managing symptoms

5-ASA Medications

Mesalamine, sulfasalazine - anti-inflammatory agents

Corticosteroids

Prednisone, budesonide - reduce inflammation

Immunosuppressants

Azathioprine, 6-mercaptopurine - reduce immune response

Biologics

Infliximab, adalimumab - target specific immune proteins

Never Stop Medications

Never stop or change your child's medications without consulting your IBD team, even if symptoms improve. Sudden discontinuation can trigger severe flares.

Emergency Situations

Call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room if your child experiences:

  • ✓ Severe abdominal pain
  • ✓ Uncontrolled bleeding
  • ✓ High fever with severe symptoms
  • ✓ Signs of severe dehydration
  • ✓ Difficulty breathing
  • ✓ Vomiting blood
